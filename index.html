In 2011, over a million of Egyptians
took to Tahrir Square, overthrowing
the 30 year rule of Hosni Mubarak.

However, in 2013 protests in Egypt
were effectively banned under
emergency laws.

Yet today, thousands of Egyptians continue to take to the streets every month in spite of the threat of violence or arrest by authorities.

Meet the protesting groups who are taking to the streets.


Muslim
Brotherhood
By our forearms we will regain our revolution

Muslim Brotherhood Group describes itself as an “inclusive reformist” movement. It is the largest political opposition movement in many Arab countries. Some political regimes, including the Egyptian one, classify it as a “terrorist group”.



Students
Chaos or reinstating the dismissed students

This group includes all the students in schools and universities in Egypt generally, who belong to different political currents and are engaged in the political life.


Labor &
Social
Where are our salaries

This group includes all the workers in different Egyptian cities, who call for their social, economic and labor rights through protesting.


Civil
Powers
Freedom for the detainees

Unlike Muslim Brotherhood, this group includes all civil and political movements that protest for political reasons, most of them are pro-democracy ones.


Pro-
Regime
The people want the excecution of Muslim Brotherhood

This group refers to the pro-regime supporters who protest to show their support to the government’s decisions that are strongly denied by the political powers. The state encourages this kind of protests at some times.

In 2015 there were 544 protests.

164 were attacked.

Ahmed Abdel Rahman
“Shura Council Incidents” Case

On 23 February 2015 Ahmed Abdel Rahman was handed down a 5-year in prison sentence, a fine of EGP100000; in addition to surveillance for a similar period after serving his prison term, over the “Shura Council Incidents” case.


Alaa Abdel Fattah
“Shura Council Incidents” Case

Alaa Abdel Fattah, on 23 February 2015, was sentenced to 5 years in jail, a fine of EGP100000, and surveillance for five years after serving his prison term. He is accused of breaching the Anti-Protest Law through organizing an unlicensed protest.


Ahmed Douma
“Cabinet Council Incidents” Case

Ahmed Douma was handed down a life sentence. He is accused of rallying, possessing cold weapons, assaulting police and military agents, setting fire to Egypt’s Scientific Community, and attacking other governmental facilities, including the Cabinet and Shura Councils.
